What exactly is NIOS?

The National Institute of Open Schooling (NIOS) is an autonomous board operating under the Ministry of Education (MHRD), Government of India.

It holds the exact same validity as CBSE, ICSE, or State Boards.

If for any reason you could not pursue regular studies or failed your exams, NIOS provides a legal, government-approved opportunity to pass your 10th or 12th standard from home with flexible classes.

What is NIOS?

Understanding NIOS — National Institute of Open Schooling

NIOS (National Institute of Open Schooling) is an autonomous board under India's Ministry of Education (MHRD). It is the world's largest open schooling system. The NIOS certificate holds the same value as CBSE, GSEB, or ICSE — accepted by all Indian universities, government recruitment boards, and even for study-abroad visa applications.

Who can apply?

Eligibility for NIOS Admission 2026

Secondary — Class 10th
Age: 14 years or above
Qualification: Class 8th pass
Board fail students: Direct eligible
Sr. Secondary — Class 12th
Age: 15 years or above
Qualification: Class 10th pass
TOC facility: Available for board fails
Key Benefits

Why Choose NIOS in 2026?

  • No daily attendance required — study from home at your own pace
  • On-Demand Exam (ODE) — give exams any time of the year, computer-based
  • TOC Facility — transfer credit of passed subjects from CBSE or GSEB boards
  • Affordable fees — much lower than private schools or repeat year costs
  • Valid for UPSC, Railway, Army, Police, Bank jobs and college admissions
  • Available in Gujarati, Hindi and English medium
